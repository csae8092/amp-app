I was<lb></lb> rather worried as to how you were, and am delighted to hear that<lb></lb> you're on the ascent after a "decline." I'm also abashed not to have<lb></lb> been in touch with you before about the collapse of our four-memoir<lb></lb> book, but the whole thing has been farcical and uncertain. Here are<lb></lb> the basics. First, there should be no need to repay any of the<lb></lb> advance (I haven't spoken with Random House about this, except with<lb></lb> an ill-informed assitant, but there shouldn't be any problem), and<lb></lb> second, I hope you haven't gone out of your way to work on the thing<lb></lb> again. The structure collapsed in two or three stages. First Lincoln<lb></lb> Kirstein read the forthcoming biography by Humphrey Carpenter (not bad,<lb></lb> not spectacularly good), and decided he didn't know enough Auden to<lb></lb> add new facts, that he wasn't really worthy to be a memoirist, etc.<lb></lb> None of this has any truth, but Kirstein is not a person to argue with.<lb></lb> He managed to persuade me, in September, that the book should at least<lb></lb> be left to stew for a while. Then Spender finally sent in a first<lb></lb> draft of his piece, which, between us, was a disaster. At this time<lb></lb> Charles Monteith had a look at it, and agreed. When I saw Charles<lb></lb> in London a couple of weeks ago, we formally let the thing drop, and<lb></lb> I think he already wrote you about it.<lb></lb> I still think your piece is first-rate (the copyright is yours, by the<lb></lb> way), and I hope you'll publish it, perhaps with a word or two of<lb></lb> information from Christa Esders if that's possible. (Did you learn<lb></lb> anything from her??) If you want me to try to place it in this country,<lb></lb> I'll do my best with it. Do let me know. I feel very sorry that you<lb></lb> went to all this trouble for--up to now--nothing, and for many reasons<lb></lb> I should like to see the piece in print.<lb></lb> No, I haven't got to Vienna for five years now, and couldn't afford<lb></lb> a visit this year. Next summer, if my job is sorted out, I would very<lb></lb> much like to come over again. This past summer I tried ringing you a<lb></lb> few times at 4217175, but got no reply. Is that your number? Mine here<lb></lb> is 212 222-0679 if there's ever a reason to need it.<lb></lb> Please do forgive me for the muddle over the book. And please do keep<lb></lb> in touch. Incidentally I finished that critical book--to be published<lb></lb> in the spring--after doing nothing but work on it for a year. My "summer"<lb></lb> trip to England got postponed to November while I revised.<lb></lb> Yours ever,<lb></lb> Ed </p> <pb facs="https://iiif.acdh.oeaw.ac.at/amp/amp_0228/" type="sheet"></pb> <p> Edward Mendelson<lb></lb> 39 Claremont Ave apt 32<lb></lb> New York NY 10027 </p> <p> Frl Baronin<lb></lb> Stella Musulin<lb></lb> Schlösselgasse 8/17<lb></lb> A-1080 Wien<lb></lb> Austria </p> </div> </body> </text> </TEI>
